Learn more about Icelandic horses!

Icelandic horses are one of the world's oldest and purest horse breeds! They were brought to Iceland by Viking settlers over a thousand years ago. Due to Iceland's strict laws prohibiting the import of foreign horses, the breed has remained unchanged for centuries.

Despite their small size — standing between 13 and 14 hands tall — Icelandic horses are not considered ponies. They are remarkably strong, hardy, and sure-footed, built to survive Iceland's harsh volcanic terrain and brutal winters. Their thick double coats keep them warm even in the most severe conditions.

One of the most unique features of the Icelandic horse is that it has five gaits rather than the usual three. In addition to the walk, trot, and canter, they also have a gait called the "tölt," which is a smooth four-beat gait that is incredibly comfortable for riders, and the "flying pace," a fast two-beat gait used in racing that can reach speeds of up to 30 miles per hour.

Icelandic horses are known for being friendly, curious, and social animals. They have a special place in Icelandic culture and are considered a national symbol of the country.

The Five Gaits of the Icelandic Horse:

  1. Walk — a slow, four-beat gait
  2. Trot — a two-beat diagonal gait
  3. Canter — a three-beat gait
  4. Tölt — a smooth four-beat gait unique to the breed
  5. Flying Pace — a fast two-beat racing gait
