Hi! My name is Maya Ellis.

I am currently a third year student at Northeastern studying Data Science and Economics, and I'm from the San Francisco Bay Area. I enjoy baking, reading, playing piano, and boxing during my free time. Some of my favorite things to bake are scones, sourdough bread, and fun cookies. I recently made raspberry chocolate chunk cookies using some of my sourdough starter discard, and they were very delicious! I also love to travel. I have studied abroad in Prague and Belgium during my time at Northeastern so far, and I'm doing another study abroad program in China this summer. After the program, I will have visited 21 countries! Over spring break last week, I went to Iceland with a couple of my friends. We rented a car and drove along the south coast of the country, since the roads farther north were facing more closures due to inclement weather. The highlight of the trip for me was glacier climbing. We put on crampons and got to climb up the ice, which was quite challenging at first but really fun! We also did an ice cave tour and had to hike on a glacier in order to get to the cave. I found it really cool, and the ice was incredibly blue. Another fun activity was horseback riding! Icelandic horses are super cute and fluffy, and there are lots of them along the roads. We booked a riding tour at a small stable and had a great time!
